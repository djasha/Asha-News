# Public Guide: Conflict Operations Dashboard

Last updated: 2026-03-02

This guide explains what the `/conflicts` dashboard shows and how to interpret it responsibly.

## What You Can See

1. Conflict-level and actor-level comparisons.
2. Verified-first event metrics (fatalities, injured, IDs released, announcements).
3. Timeline plus comparative trend overlays (events, fatalities, injured, IDs, official announcements).
4. Location/weapon/technology trend summaries.
5. Related news ranked by signal overlap, recency, and source quality.
6. Analyst tab with hypotheses and probability forecasts (when enabled).

## Verification Labels

1. `verified`: reviewed/corroborated under current criteria.
2. `unverified`: reported but not yet verified.
3. `rejected`: reviewed and not accepted under verification rules.

Default dashboard mode is verified-first.

## Related News Ranking (Simplified)

Ranking combines:

1. Signal overlap with conflict events.
2. Recency.
3. Source tier.
4. Verification alignment.

Ties are broken by newest timestamp, higher source tier, and higher signal diversity.

## Identities and Privacy

Names/identities are intentionally restricted.

If verification and policy checks do not pass, identity details are withheld and only counts may be shown.

## Theories and Forecasts

1. Theory cards are hypotheses, not facts.
2. Forecast cards are probabilistic scenarios, not certainty statements.
3. All such content should be interpreted with uncertainty and changing conditions in mind.

## Important Limitations

1. Conflict data can change rapidly.
2. Verification can lag event reporting.
3. AI-generated analysis can be wrong or incomplete.
4. Dashboard output should be combined with primary-source review.
